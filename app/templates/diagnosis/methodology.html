How Diagnosis Works

Understanding our ingredient-symptom correlation analysis

Overview

Our diagnosis feature analyzes your meal and symptom data to identify ingredients that may be associated with your health symptoms. This analysis is based on temporal patterns, statistical correlations, and medical research.

Temporal Lag Windows

We analyze symptoms that occur within different time windows after eating:

  • Immediate (0-2 hours): Typical of allergic reactions and intolerances
  • Delayed (4-24 hours): Common for digestive issues and IBS triggers
  • Cumulative (24+ hours): Inflammatory responses and long-term effects

These windows are grounded in medical research on how different types of food reactions manifest over time.

Confidence Scoring

We calculate a confidence score for each ingredient-symptom correlation based on:

  • Statistical confidence (50% weight): How often symptoms follow consumption
  • Temporal specificity (30% weight): Concentration in specific time windows
  • Severity weighting (20% weight): Average severity of associated symptoms

Confidence levels:

  • ● HIGH: ≥70% confidence
  • ● MEDIUM: 40-69% confidence
  • ● LOW: <40% confidence

Medical Grounding

Our AI analysis uses web search to find relevant medical research and provide context for identified correlations. We prioritize sources from:

  • NIH (National Institutes of Health)
  • PubMed and peer-reviewed medical journals
  • Registered dietitian organizations
  • Academic and medical institutions (.edu, .org domains)

All sources are cited with hyperlinks so you can verify the information.

Important Limitations

This analysis has important limitations:

  • Correlation ≠ Causation: Just because symptoms follow eating an ingredient doesn't mean the ingredient caused the symptoms
  • Confounding factors: Many things influence symptoms (stress, sleep, other foods, medications, etc.)
  • Sample size: Results improve with more data. Minimum 5 meals and 3 symptoms required
  • Individual variation: Food responses are highly personal and may not match typical patterns
  • Not medical advice: This tool does NOT diagnose conditions or replace professional medical consultation

What To Do With Results

If the analysis identifies potential trigger ingredients:

  1. Discuss with a healthcare professional: Share the results with your doctor, gastroenterologist, or registered dietitian for proper evaluation
  2. Consider an elimination trial: With medical supervision, try removing the ingredient for 2-3 weeks, then reintroduce it to observe effects
  3. Continue tracking: More data improves accuracy. Keep logging meals and symptoms
  4. Try different preparations: If state matters (raw vs cooked), experiment with different cooking methods
  5. Provide feedback: Rate the results to help us improve the analysis
